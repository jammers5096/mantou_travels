Understanding Your Hair Type

Jam | Hair Styles 12 November 2024

The first secret to unlocking flawless hair is understanding your hair type!

Your hair’s curl pattern determines your hair type. That will determine whether your hair is straight, wavy, curly, or coily/kinky.

Here’s an easy way to check what your hair type is:

The next time you wash your hair, let it air dry with no products or heat applied.

- Straight hair will dry straight without bending or curling.
- Wavy hair will dry in an “S” shape.
- Curly hair will dry with a defined curl.
- Coily/kinky hair will dry in tight curls or spirals.

Once you know your hair type, try following a dedicated hair care routine that fits your type!

For straight hair, the priority is to control greasiness and maintain your sheen. Since straight hair gets greasy easily, use a sulfate-free shampoo that can remove excess grease and dirt in your hair.

Avoid leave-in conditioners if you have straight hair, and only apply conditioner to the ends of your hair rather than the roots. Try to use a light conditioner that won’t weigh down your hair too much.

If you have wavy hair, you can focus on enhancing and defining your waves. Opt for a sulphate-free, hydrating shampoo and leave-in conditioner without harsh chemicals.

Avoid rough towels when drying your hair. Instead, scrunch out the remaining moisture with a soft microfiber towel or soft cotton shirt. After applying styling products like light mousse or gel and a heat protectant, you can use our Flowé hair dryer on low heat to fully dry and style your hair.

Tip: Try sleeping on a silk pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep!

Frizz is a common occurrence for curly hair, so the key is to retain moisture and combat dryness in your hair. Wash your hair only once a week to keep the natural oils in your curls. Always use a heat protectant before using styling tools like our Flowé hair dryer or any straighteners and curlers.

Go for regular hair trims to avoid split ends in your hair! And just like with wavy hair, do not use rough towels when drying your hair.

Tip: Use cold water when washing your hair! Hot water can result in more frizz.

Finally, if you have coily hair, preventing breakage is the most important goal. Protective hairstyles like box braids, bantu knots, and twist-outs can help keep your coils protected. As with curly and wavy hair, you can try using silk pillowcases to protect your hair.

You can try a technique called “co-washing” where you use a conditioner and no shampoo to wash your hair to avoid the harsh chemicals from shampoos while keeping your hair clean.

Now that you have a better understanding of your hair type and how to care for it, you can leave your house every day with flawless hair and endless confidence!
